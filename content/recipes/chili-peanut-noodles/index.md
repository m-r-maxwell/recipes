### 560 Calories | 34g Protein | 45g Carbs | 26g Fat
### Serving Size: 4

### Ingredients:
- 800g ground beef (lean recommended, ~85–90%)
- 1 packet gluten-free noodles (rice noodles or GF spaghetti ~250–300g dry)
- 1 red onion, sliced
- 1 bell pepper, sliced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp ground ginger
- 2 tbsp creamy peanut butter
- 2 tbsp gluten-free soy sauce or tamari
- 1 tbsp chili paste or sriracha (adjust to heat preference)
- 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar or lime juice
- 1/3 cup water (to loosen sauce)
- 1 tsp chili powder
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- Optional for topping: green onions, crushed peanuts, sesame seeds


### Steps:
1. Cook noodles according to package instructions, then drain and set aside. Toss lightly with oil if needed to prevent sticking.
2. In a large pan over medium-high heat, cook the ground beef until browned and fully cooked. Drain excess fat if necessary.
3. Add the sliced onion and bell pepper to the pan. Cook for 4–5 minutes until softened.
4. Stir in garlic and ground ginger, cooking for about 1 minute until fragrant.
5. In a small bowl, whisk together peanut butter, soy sauce, chili paste, honey, vinegar, water, chili powder, paprika, salt, and pepper until smooth.
6. Pour the sauce into the pan with the beef and vegetables. Stir well and let it simmer for 2–3 minutes until slightly thickened.
7. Add the cooked noodles and toss everything together until evenly coated. Add a splash of water if the sauce is too thick.
8. Taste and adjust heat, salt, or sweetness as needed.

### Serving:
Divide into 4 meal prep containers and top with optional garnishes if desired.
